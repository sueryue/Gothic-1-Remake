❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common Gothic 1 Remake questions

General Gameplay Factions Combat Lockpicking Quests Technical Story & Lore

📚 General Questions

What is Gothic 1 Remake?
Gothic 1 Remake is a full rebuild of the 2001 classic RPG by Alkimia Interactive, published by THQ Nordic. It keeps the original's open-world design, faction system, and gritty atmosphere while bringing modern graphics, updated combat, and expanded questlines. Released June 5, 2026 on PC (Steam).
Do I need to play the original Gothic first?
No. The remake is designed for both newcomers and veterans. The story, tutorials, and progression have been modernized so first-time players can jump right in. If you played the original, you'll recognize the world and characters but find plenty of new content.
How long is the game?
A single playthrough takes about 40-55 hours for the main story plus side content. Completionist runs can reach 70+ hours. Each faction has its own questline, so replaying with a different faction adds another 30-40 hours per run.
Is there multiplayer or co-op?
No. Gothic 1 Remake is a single-player RPG. There are no multiplayer or co-op modes.
What's different from the original Gothic?
The remake keeps the core DNA: open world without hand-holding, faction choice, lockpicking puzzles, and the gritty prison colony setting. New additions include: fully modernized combat system, expanded questlines, new traversal abilities (climbing, swimming improvements), additional NPC routines and reactions, updated graphics on Unreal Engine, and new content filling gaps from the original.
Is the game fully voice-acted?
Yes. Full audio support in English, German, Polish, and Russian. Subtitles available in French, Italian, Spanish, Simplified Chinese, Japanese, and Brazilian Portuguese.

🎮 Gameplay Questions

How does leveling work?
You earn XP from quests and killing enemies. When you level up, you gain Learning Points (LP). You spend LP at trainers to increase stats like Strength, Dexterity, and Mana, or to learn new skills. You can't just allocate points from a menu — you need to find the right NPC trainer for each stat or skill.
How do I heal?
Early on: eat herbs, mushrooms, and cooked meat. Later: health potions, healing spells (Swamp Camp), and food from camp cooks. There's no passive health regeneration outside of specific spells or items. Always carry food and potions before heading into dangerous areas.
Can I change the difficulty?
Yes, Gothic 1 Remake has adjustable difficulty settings that you can change at any time from the options menu. The game also supports "Save Anytime" so you can experiment with different approaches.
How do I make money?
The currency is ore coins. Earn them by: completing quests, selling monster parts and herbs to merchants, looting chests and bodies, and winning arena fights. Early game: gather and sell herbs to Chef Snuf for quick coins. Mid game: monster hunting and quest rewards become the main income source.
What happens when I die?
You reload your last save. There's no death penalty, but you lose progress since your last save. Save frequently — the game doesn't auto-save often. Keep multiple rotating save slots (at least 3).

⚔️ Faction Questions

Which faction should I choose first?
Old Camp is best for first-timers. It has the clearest quest structure, strong early armor, and fire magic. New Camp is good for a second playthrough — more freedom and unique weapons. Swamp Camp is the hardest but most unique — spirit magic and tactical gameplay. You can take the faction quiz if you're unsure.
Can I switch factions later?
Technically possible but extremely difficult and not recommended. Once you complete a faction's initiation quest, you're locked in. The game is designed for you to commit to one faction per playthrough. If you want to experience all three, plan on multiple playthroughs.
Can I join Swamp Camp after joining another faction?
No. Swamp Camp must be chosen at the start of the game. Once you join Old Camp or New Camp, Swamp Camp is permanently closed to you for that playthrough.
Does faction choice affect the ending?
Yes. Each faction has a different ending with different allies, different final battles, and different outcomes for the colony. Worth replaying to see them all.

⚔️ Combat Questions

Why do I keep dying at low levels?
This is normal. Gothic 1 Remake doesn't scale enemies to your level. At level 1-3, you should be running from everything except scavengers. The game expects you to learn which fights you can win and which you can't. Levels 4-6 you can handle wolves and molerats. By level 10 you can hold your own against most enemies. See the Bestiary for enemy difficulty ratings.
What's the best weapon type?
Depends on your faction and build. Old Camp: one-handed swords are strongest. New Camp: two-handed axes and bows excel. Swamp Camp: staves and magic. Pick one weapon type and stick with it — spreading your skills across multiple weapon types will leave you weak in all of them.
How does blocking and dodging work?
Block small, fast attacks from weaker enemies. Dodge heavy, telegraphed attacks from larger enemies. The combat is deliberate and tactical — button mashing will get you killed. Watch enemy attack patterns: each enemy type has a tell before its heavy attacks. Learning these is more important than raw stats.

🔒 Lockpicking Questions

How do puzzle locks work?
Puzzle locks have colored plates (3-7 depending on difficulty). Each button press changes some plates. Red button: changes one plate. Blue button: changes adjacent plates. Yellow button: changes an entire row. The goal is to turn all plates green without breaking any lockpicks. See the Lockpicking Guide for detailed walkthroughs.
What happens if I fail a lock?
Failed attempts waste lockpicks. If you run out of lockpicks, you can't attempt the lock again until you find or buy more. Some key locks (like the Collapsed Tower) also have a maximum attempt limit — fail too many times and the lock becomes permanently jammed. Use the Lock Solver to avoid this.
Where can I get more lockpicks?
Buy them from merchants in any camp. Thieves and New Camp members sell them at better prices. Some can be found as loot in chests or on enemy bodies. Always keep at least 10 lockpicks in your inventory before attempting a multi-plate lock.

📝 Quest Questions

Can I miss quests?
Yes. Many quests are time-sensitive or become unavailable after certain story events. Side quests in particular can be missed if you progress too far in the main story without completing them. Talk to every named NPC and complete side quests before advancing the main storyline.
Are there quest markers?
No quest markers on a minimap. NPCs give you directions verbally — pay attention to what they say. Graham sells maps with routes marked, which helps. This is intentional design: the game wants you to navigate the world yourself rather than following waypoints.
How do I track active quests?
Check your quest journal. It records what NPCs told you, including directions and objectives. The journal is your best friend — read it carefully. It contains the clues NPCs gave you, which is your navigation system.

🖥️ Technical Questions

What are the system requirements?
See the System Requirements page for full details. Minimum: i7-7700K, 16GB RAM, RTX 2070, 60GB SSD. Recommended: i7-7700K, 32GB RAM, RTX 3070 Ti, 60GB NVMe.
Does the game support controllers?
Yes. Full controller support with DualShock and DualSense compatibility. Keyboard and mouse are also fully supported — the choice is yours.
Is there Steam Cloud support?
Yes, Gothic 1 Remake supports Steam Cloud for save synchronization across devices. It also has Family Sharing support.
How do I report bugs?
Contact support@quanticlab.com or use the Steam Community forums. Check the Community page for links to official channels.
Will this run on Steam Deck?
The game requires Windows 10/11 64-bit and is not natively supported on Linux/SteamOS. Steam Deck compatibility has not been officially confirmed. Check ProtonDB for community testing results.

📖 Story & Lore Questions

Who am I playing as?
You play as the Nameless Hero — a prisoner thrown into the mining colony of Khorinis. You have no name, no backstory, and no allies. Your goal: survive, join a faction, and find a way out of the magical barrier that traps everyone inside.
What is the magical barrier?
King Rhobar II ordered his mages to create a barrier around the mining colony to prevent prisoners from escaping. The spell went wrong — instead of keeping prisoners in, it trapped EVERYONE inside, including the guards and mages. The barrier is the central mystery of the game's story.
Who is Diego?
Diego is the first friendly NPC you meet and your guide through the early game. He's a veteran of the Old Camp who escorts you there, teaches you basic stats, and serves as your main quest-giver if you choose the Old Camp path. He's one of the most important characters in the game.
Is the story connected to Gothic 2 and 3?
Yes. Gothic 1 Remake is the first game in the trilogy. Your choices and faction allegiance set up the events of Gothic 2. Characters like Diego, Gomez, and the Nameless Hero continue their stories in the sequels.
