Phase 1: The Awakening Hours 0-2

⚠️ Survival Mode: You have no weapons, no armor, and no allies. Everything can kill you. Stay alert!

Waking Up on the Shore

The game opens with you washed ashore after a shipwreck. The magical barrier looms in the distance — that's where you're heading. Look around for basic supplies.

Objectives:

  • Explore the beach area for loose items
  • Collect any berries and herbs you find (they restore health)
  • Follow the path inland — avoid any creatures you see
  • Talk to the first person you encounter
💡 Pro Tip: At level 1, you can't fight anything. Running away is not cowardice — it's survival. Pick up everything: herbs, mushrooms, raw meat. They're your only source of healing and early income.

Meeting Diego

You'll encounter Diego, a seasoned veteran who becomes your guide. He offers to escort you to the Old Camp. Always accept his help.

Why Diego Matters:

  • He protects you from mobs during the journey
  • He explains the world's lore, factions, and politics
  • He teaches Strength & Agility stats later on
  • He's your main quest-giver for the Old Camp path
💡 Pro Tip: Listen to what Diego says. His dialogue explains the factions, the politics, and who's who. NPC conversations are how the game teaches you — there are no pop-up tutorials.

Arriving at Old Camp

Diego brings you to the Old Camp — the largest settlement in the colony. This is your first safe zone. Use this time to learn the layout and meet key NPCs.

Key NPCs to Meet:

  • Diego — Stats trainer (Strength, Agility)
  • Skirti — One-handed weapon training
  • Torres — Rune magic, mana upgrades, magical items
  • Graham — Sells maps with routes marked
  • Wesley — Guard captain, gives "Sword of Sentry" quest
  • Chef Snuf — Cooking and food supplies

Phase 2: First Steps in the Colony Hours 2-5

📌 Goal: Reach level 5 and gather resources before making any permanent decisions.

Early Questing & Leveling

Take on basic quests to earn XP, ore coins, and reputation. These early missions teach core mechanics without being too punishing.

Priority Quest Order:

  • Cooking Ingredients: Collect 3 meat worms + 5 mushrooms for Chef Snuf
  • Missing Guard: Find guard Nick (learns about patrol routes)
  • Herb Gathering: Collect healing plants around the camp perimeter
  • Arena Training: Challenge Cole and Kyle in the arena (safe combat practice)
  • Find Pinks: Locate the stealth skill teacher
💡 Pro Tip: The arena is the safest place to practice combat. You can't die there, and you'll learn timing and enemy patterns. Spend at least 30 minutes here before venturing out.

Exploring the Colony

Now that you're level 3-4, it's safe to explore between camps. Visit all three faction headquarters before committing to one.

Travel Routes (Verified):

  • Old Camp to New Camp: Exit south gate, follow western road toward lake. Pass Cavalorn's cabin then Aidan's cabin. Continue west to the lake — New Camp sits on the hill.
  • Old Camp to Swamp Camp: More dangerous route through marshland. Level 5+ recommended.
⚠️ Warning: Travel between camps at levels 1-3 is dangerous. Wolves, bandits, and beasts patrol the roads. Minimum level 4-5 recommended. Save before every journey.

Phase 3: Choosing Your Path Hours 5-10

⚠️ Point of No Return: Your faction choice is PERMANENT. Complete quests for ALL THREE camps before committing!

Evaluating All Three Factions

Before choosing, you should complete the intro quests for each camp. This gives you XP, loot, and a feel for each faction's culture.

🔥 Old Camp (Best for Beginners)

  • Easiest intro quests with structured progression
  • Access to fire magic through Torres
  • Premium armor available early game
  • Well-organized hierarchy with clear direction

💧 New Camp (Combat-Focused)

  • Two-handed weapon training available early
  • Unique powerful weapons not available elsewhere
  • Independent, less hierarchical society
  • Higher difficulty early, better late-game rewards

🌀 Swamp Camp (Magic-Focused)

  • Unique Spirit Magic: Sleep, Telekinesis, Wind Hammer
  • Perfect for tactical spell-casters
  • Steepest learning curve but most unique gameplay
  • Only available at game start — cannot join later

Phase 4: Faction Initiation Hours 10-20

Old Camp Initiation Path

Requirements:

  • Reach level 5+
  • Complete 6 main quests (Missing Guard, Chef's Worms, Arena Challenges, Find Pinks)
  • Get support from at least 4 Shadows members
  • Pass Diego's test — retrieve supply list from Old Mine
  • Meet Gomez in castle, name 4 valid contacts

Rewards:

  • Shadow Armor (unique faction armor)
  • Large XP boost
  • Ore coins as currency
  • Access to Inner Circle treasures

New Camp Initiation Path

Requirements:

  • Reach level 5+
  • Modify and deliver supply list to Lares
  • Acquire Mordrag's ring
  • Gather mineral/resources for various tasks
  • Earn trust of key characters
  • Collaborate with Rice Lord
  • Participate in supply convoy ambush (NEW in Remake)

Swamp Camp Initiation Path

Requirements:

  • Reach level 5+
  • Impress 4 spiritual guides (NOT military leaders)
  • Each guide demands different proof: ingenuity, collection tasks, magical capability
  • Once all 4 favor you, accepted as novice
  • Begin path toward Templar ranks
💡 Pro Tip: Swamp Camp has NO second chance. If you choose another faction first, you cannot switch. Decide before completing any other faction's final initiation quest.

Phase 5: Rising in Power Hours 20-35

Mid-Game Priorities

You're in a faction now. Access to better trainers, gear, and quests opens up. Time to get stronger and push into tougher areas.

Key Activities:

  • Weapon Mastery: Train with your faction's weapon specialist. Old Camp: one-handed. New Camp: two-handed. Swamp Camp: magic-enhanced combat.
  • Armor Upgrades: Complete faction quests to earn better armor tiers.
  • Spell Acquisition: Old Camp — fire runes. New Camp — water ring (freeze). Swamp Camp — Sleep, Telekinesis.
  • Lockpicking: Learn lockpicking to access hidden treasures. Use the solver for tough locks.
  • Map Purchase: Buy from Graham (Old Camp) after talking to Wesley for marked routes.

Intermediate & Advanced Locks

As you explore deeper areas, you'll encounter 5-7 plate puzzle locks. These guard the best loot in the game.

Lock Difficulty Reference:

  • 5-Plate locks: Near orc camp, mountain passage east of White Camp
  • 6-7 Plate locks: Advanced areas, pre-Collapsed Tower zones
  • Collapsed Tower Lock: Most complex — 57 steps, 7 plates with mechanical linkages

Phase 6: Endgame Hours 35-50+

Endgame Preparation

You're nearing the end. Make sure your gear is maxed out and you're happy with your build — there's no turning back.

Pre-Endgame Checklist:

  • All faction quests completed
  • Best available armor equipped
  • Primary weapon skill maxed
  • Key spells learned (based on faction)
  • Repair kits and healing items stocked
  • Collapsed Tower lock solved (if applicable)
  • Map fully explored

The Final Confrontation

The endgame is about the magical barrier — what it is, who built it, and how to get out. Each faction's ending is different: different allies, different fights, different outcomes.

⚔️ Final Battle Tips: Make multiple save files before the last fight. Each faction gets its own ending — worth replaying to see them all.

Quick Reference Cards

💾 Save Management

Manual saves are permanent. Quicksave (Q key) is overwritten. Keep at least 3 rotating save slots. Save before every lock attempt, faction decision, and boss encounter.

📈 Leveling Strategy

Focus on one weapon type. Don't spread stats evenly. Old/New Camp: prioritize Strength. Swamp Camp: prioritize Mana. Always keep enough LP for emergency skills.

🛡️ Survival Rules

Level 1-3: flee everything. Level 4-6: fight only weaker enemies (scavengers, young wolves). Level 7+: engage most enemies. Level 10+: you can hold your own.

🔐 Lock Priority

Training Center (1-4 plate) → World locks (5 plate) → Collapsed Tower (7 plate). Use the solver to avoid lock damage.
